# MODULE 01: LANDING PAGE - "Understanding Risk for Decision-Making in Tanzania"

```
HAZARD → EXPOSURE → SENSITIVITY → VULNERABILITY → COPING CAPACITY → RISK
```

## PART A: CORE CONCEPTS (6 Sequential Sections)

### 1️⃣ HAZARD - "What Can Happen?"

**Section Title:** "Hazards in Tanzania: Events That May Occur"

**INFORM Definition:**
> A hazard is a potentially damaging physical or human-induced event.

**Key Principle:**
> **Hazard alone does NOT create risk.**
> A flood in an uninhabited area has zero humanitarian risk.

**Content Structure:**

#### Natural Hazards
- 🌧️ Heavy rainfall
- 🌊 Floods (riverine & flash)
- ☀️ Drought
- 🌪️ Cyclones
- 🌊 Large waves (coastal)
- 🔥 Wildfires
- 🌡️ Extreme temperatures
- 🌡️ Heat waves

#### Human Hazards
- ⚔️ Conflict & unrest
- 🦠 Epidemics & disease outbreaks

#### Geological Hazards
- 🌋 Volcanic activity
- 🏔️ Earthquakes
- ⛰️ Landslides

**Visual Elements:**

1. **Hazard Map** (No population yet)
   - Show historical hazard occurrences
   - Frequency data (last 10 years)
   - Probability zones (where available)

2. **Timeline Visualization**
   - Major hazard events in Tanzania (1980-2024)
   - Event frequency graph

3. **Teaching Box:**
   ```
   ⚠️ CRITICAL LESSON
   ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
   HAZARD ≠ DISASTER

   A hazard becomes a disaster only when:
   • People are exposed
   • Communities are vulnerable
   • Response capacity is inadequate
   ```

**NO impact mentioned yet**
**NO population mentioned yet**

This section teaches: **"Events exist, but they don't automatically cause crises"**

### 2️⃣ EXPOSURE - "Who or What is in Harm's Way?"

**Section Title:** "Exposure: Where Hazards Meet People"

**INFORM Definition:**
> Exposure is the presence of people, infrastructure, or livelihoods in hazard-prone areas.

**INFORM Formula Concept:**

Exposure is expressed as:
- **Absolute exposure**: Number of people in hazard zones
- **Relative exposure**: Percentage of population in hazard zones

INFORM uses both to avoid bias toward large or small populations.

**Content Structure:**

#### The Overlay Concept

```
┌─────────────┐     ┌─────────────┐     ┌─────────────┐
│   Hazard    │  +  │ Population  │  =  │  Exposure   │
│    Zone     │     │ Distribution│     │             │
└─────────────┘     └─────────────┘     └─────────────┘
```

#### Exposure Indicators (Tanzania)

**Example Display:**

| District | Flood Hazard Zone (km²) | Population in Zone | Relative Exposure (%) |
|----------|------------------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Dar es Salaam | 450 | 1,200,000 | 65% |
| Dodoma | 200 | 150,000 | 25% |

**Visual Elements:**

1. **Interactive Overlay Map**
   - Layer 1: Hazard zones (toggleable by hazard type)
   - Layer 2: Population density heatmap
   - Layer 3: Combined exposure (overlay)

2. **Exposure Metrics Dashboard**
   - Total population in multi-hazard zones
   - Urban vs rural exposure
   - Coastal exposure (sea level rise)

3. **Teaching Box:**
   ```
   🧠 KEY LESSON
   ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
   Tanzania's Challenge:

   Moderate hazards + High exposure
   = Significant potential impact

   Example: Dar es Salaam
   • Moderate flood hazard
   • 65% of population in flood zone
   • = Very high flood exposure
   ```

### 3️⃣ SENSITIVITY - "Why the Same Hazard Causes Different Impacts?"

**Section Title:** "Sensitivity: How Severely People Are Affected"

**Scientific Definition:**
> Sensitivity is how strongly exposed people are affected when a hazard occurs.

**INFORM Context:**

⚠️ Note: INFORM embeds sensitivity within Vulnerability (socio-economic & vulnerable groups)

We must explain this explicitly to avoid confusion.

**Content Structure:**

#### Sensitivity Factors

**Same Hazard, Different Outcomes:**

```
District A                    District B
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━           ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Same Rainfall (100mm)         Same Rainfall (100mm)
    ↓                             ↓
Poor Housing                  Strong Housing
Malnutrition                  Good Nutrition
Weak Health                   Good Health
No Drainage                   Good Drainage
    ↓                             ↓
HIGH IMPACT                   LOW IMPACT
(Disaster)                    (Manageable)
```

#### Sensitivity Indicators (Tanzania)

1. **Housing Quality**
   - % mud/thatch housing
   - % concrete/permanent housing

2. **Health Status**
   - Malnutrition rates
   - Disease prevalence
   - Access to healthcare

3. **Infrastructure**
   - Drainage systems
   - Road quality
   - Water systems

4. **Economic Status**
   - Poverty rates
   - Income levels
   - Livelihood diversity

**Visual Elements:**

1. **Comparative Case Study**
   - Two districts with same flood exposure
   - Side-by-side impact comparison
   - Show sensitivity factors causing difference

2. **Sensitivity Heat Map**
   - Color-coded by sensitivity level
   - Multiple sensitivity indicators overlay

3. **Teaching Box:**
   ```
   💡 CRITICAL INSIGHT
   ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
   "Disasters Are Not Natural"

   Natural hazards are inevitable.
   But DISASTERS are created by:
   • Poverty
   • Poor infrastructure
   • Weak health systems
   • Inequality

   → Reducing sensitivity reduces disaster impact
   ```

This section teaches: **"Vulnerability makes hazards disastrous"**

### 4️⃣ VULNERABILITY - "How Susceptible Are Communities?"

**Section Title:** "Vulnerability: Why Some Communities Suffer More"

**INFORM Definition:**
> Vulnerability is the susceptibility of communities to hazards.

**INFORM Structure:**

Vulnerability has **TWO categories**:

1. **Socio-Economic Vulnerability**
   - Development & deprivation
   - Inequality
   - Aid dependency

2. **Vulnerable Groups**
   - Children under 5
   - Elderly (>65)
   - Persons with Disabilities (PWDs)
   - Displaced populations
   - Women in vulnerable situations

**INFORM Mathematical Role:**

Vulnerability is **one of three equal dimensions** in the risk equation.

**Content Structure:**

#### Component 1: Socio-Economic Vulnerability

**Indicators:**

| Category | Indicators (Tanzania) |
|----------|---------------------|
| Poverty | % below poverty line, Asset ownership |
| Food Security | Malnutrition rates, Food production index |
| Health | Maternal mortality, Disease burden |
| Education | Literacy rate, School enrollment |
| Water & Sanitation | Access to clean water, Improved sanitation |

#### Component 2: Vulnerable Groups

**Population Segments:**

| Group | Indicator | National % | Why Vulnerable |
|-------|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| Children <5 | 0-4 population | ~15% | Physical weakness, dependency |
| Elderly >65 | 65+ population | ~4% | Mobility, health issues |
| PWDs | Disability prevalence | ~7% | Mobility, access barriers |
| Displaced | IDP/refugee count | Variable | Loss of assets, networks |

**Visual Elements:**

1. **Vulnerability Profile Dashboard**
   - National vulnerability score (0-10)
   - Breakdown by category
   - District-level comparison

2. **Vulnerable Groups Population Map**
   - Heat map showing concentration of vulnerable groups
   - Overlayable layers per group type

3. **Historical Impact Correlation**
   - Graph showing: High vulnerability areas = Higher past disaster impacts
   - Reinforces the concept

4. **INFORM Formula Reveal (First Time):**
   ```
   ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
   │  INFORM RISK EQUATION (1 of 3 dimensions)  │
   ├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
   │                                             │
   │  Risk = (H&E)^(1/3) × (V)^(1/3) × (LCC)^(1/3) │
   │                        ↑                    │
   │                        │                    │
   │                  VULNERABILITY              │
   │                                             │
   │  Where:                                     │
   │  • V exists BEFORE disasters                │
   │  • Reducing V reduces future risk           │
   │  • V is preventable through development     │
   └─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
   ```

5. **Teaching Box:**
   ```
   📌 KEY EMPHASIS
   ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
   Vulnerability Is Pre-Existing

   ✓ Exists before disasters
   ✓ Can be measured and monitored
   ✓ Can be reduced through development
   ✓ Reduction = Prevention

   Example: Tanzania
   • Improving nutrition = Lower drought vulnerability
   • Better housing = Lower flood vulnerability
   • Stronger health = Lower epidemic vulnerability
   ```

This section teaches: **"Vulnerability is a root cause, not a consequence"**

### 5️⃣ COPING / ADAPTIVE CAPACITY - "Can Impacts Be Managed?"

**Section Title:** "Coping Capacity: Ability to Prepare, Respond, and Recover"

**INFORM Term:**
> "Lack of Coping Capacity"

We explain **both sides**: Capacity (positive) vs Lack (negative in formula)

**INFORM Definition:**
> Coping capacity is the ability of systems, institutions, and communities to reduce disaster impact through:
> - **Preparedness**: Early warning, planning
> - **Response**: Emergency services, relief
> - **Recovery**: Reconstruction, resilience

**Components (INFORM Categories):**

1. **Institutional Capacity**
   - Disaster Risk Reduction (DRR) governance
   - Quality of governance
   - Corruption perception

2. **Infrastructure**
   - Communication systems
   - Physical infrastructure
   - Access to healthcare

3. **Health Services**
   - Hospitals per capita
   - Trained medical staff
   - Emergency response capacity

**Key INFORM Insight:**
> Even highly exposed and vulnerable populations may avoid crisis if coping capacity is strong.

**Content Structure:**

#### Coping Capacity Framework

```
COPING CAPACITY = Ability to:
    ↓
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│  PREPARE   │  RESPOND   │  RECOVER  │
├────────────┼────────────┼───────────┤
│ • Early    │ • Emergency│ • Rebuild │
│   warning  │   services │   better  │
│ • Planning │ • Relief   │ • Learn   │
│ • Training │ • Search & │ • Adapt   │
│            │   rescue   │           │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

#### Coping Capacity Indicators (Tanzania)

**Institutional:**
- Existence of National Disaster Management Authority (PMO-DMD) ✓
- District Disaster Committees
- DRR budget allocation
- Emergency response SOPs

**Infrastructure:**
- Early warning system coverage
- Road accessibility (all-season)
- Communication network coverage
- Emergency shelters

**Health:**
- Hospitals per 100,000 people
- Ambulance availability
- Blood bank capacity
- Disease surveillance systems

**Visual Elements:**

1. **Capacity vs Lack Comparison**
   ```
   High Coping Capacity        Low Coping Capacity
   ═══════════════════        ═══════════════════
   Same hazard                Same hazard
   Same exposure              Same exposure
   Same vulnerability         Same vulnerability
        ↓                           ↓
   Managed situation          CRISIS
   (Affected)                 (Overwhelmed)
   ```

2. **Tanzania Coping Capacity Map**
   - Color-coded by district capacity level
   - Show infrastructure density
   - Emergency service coverage

3. **Historical Response Analysis**
   - Past disasters
   - Response effectiveness
   - Lessons learned

4. **INFORM Formula (Second Dimension):**
   ```
   ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
   │  INFORM RISK EQUATION (2 of 3 dimensions)  │
   ├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
   │                                             │
   │  Risk = (H&E)^(1/3) × (V)^(1/3) × (LCC)^(1/3) │
   │                                      ↑      │
   │                                      │      │
   │                        LACK OF COPING CAPACITY │
   │                                             │
   │  Where:                                     │
   │  • LCC = Inverse of coping capacity         │
   │  • Strong capacity = Low LCC = Lower risk   │
   │  • LCC can be improved through investment   │
   └─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
   ```

5. **Teaching Box:**
   ```
   🧠 TEACHING POINT
   ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
   Coping Capacity Counterbalances Vulnerability

   Scenario: High Vulnerability District

   WITHOUT Coping:          WITH Coping:
   Drought → Famine         Drought → Managed food distribution
   Epidemic → Deaths        Epidemic → Controlled outbreak
   Flood → Displacement     Flood → Safe evacuation

   → Investing in capacity = Risk reduction
   ```

This section teaches: **"Capacity determines whether a hazard becomes a crisis"**

### 6️⃣ RISK - "Probability of Humanitarian Crisis"

**Section Title:** "Risk: Combining All Dimensions"

**INFORM Definition:**
> Risk is NOT impact.
> Risk is the **likelihood of a crisis overwhelming national capacity**.

**Full INFORM Risk Equation (Explicitly Shown):**

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│          INFORM RISK FORMULA                         │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                      │
│  Risk = (H&E)^(1/3) × (V)^(1/3) × (LCC)^(1/3)       │
│                                                      │
│  Where:                                              │
│  • H&E = Hazard & Exposure                          │
│  • V   = Vulnerability                              │
│  • LCC = Lack of Coping Capacity                    │
│                                                      │
│  Scale: 0 (No Risk) to 10 (Very High Risk)          │
│                                                      │
│  Equal Weight: Each dimension contributes equally    │
│  (Geometric mean ensures balance)                    │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Content Structure:**

#### Understanding the Formula

**Why Geometric Mean (Cube Root)?**

```
Arithmetic Mean            Geometric Mean (INFORM)
═══════════════            ═══════════════════════
(H + V + LCC) / 3          (H × V × LCC)^(1/3)

Problem:                   Solution:
High in one dimension      All dimensions must be
compensates for            addressed - no
low in others              compensation allowed

Example:
H=9, V=1, LCC=1            H=9, V=1, LCC=1
Arithmetic = 3.7           Geometric = 2.1
(Misleading - looks        (Correct - shows low
moderate)                  overall capacity)
```

#### Tanzania National Risk

**Display Elements:**

1. **Risk Score Card**
   ```
   ┌────────────────────────────┐
   │  TANZANIA NATIONAL RISK    │
   ├────────────────────────────┤
   │         Score: 4.2         │
   │    (Medium-High Risk)      │
   ├────────────────────────────┤
   │  Dimension Breakdown:      │
   │  • Hazard & Exposure: 3.8  │
   │  • Vulnerability: 5.1      │
   │  • Lack of Coping: 3.9     │
   └────────────────────────────┘
   ```

2. **Risk Level Classification**

   | Score | Level | Color | Meaning |
   |-------|-------|-------|---------|
   | 0.0 - 2.0 | Very Low | 🟢 Green | Crisis very unlikely |
   | 2.0 - 3.5 | Low | 🟡 Yellow | Crisis unlikely |
   | 3.5 - 5.0 | Medium | 🟠 Orange | Crisis possible |
   | 5.0 - 6.5 | High | 🔴 Red | Crisis likely |
   | 6.5 - 10.0 | Very High | ⚫ Dark Red | Crisis very likely |

3. **What "Medium-High Risk" Actually Means:**
   ```
   ⚠️ INTERPRETATION GUIDE
   ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
   Tanzania Score: 4.2 (Medium-High)

   This means:
   ✓ Multiple hazards exist (cyclones, droughts, floods)
   ✓ Large populations are exposed
   ✓ Significant vulnerability persists
   ✓ Response capacity exists but may be overwhelmed

   → Likelihood: Shocks will periodically exceed
                 national response capacity

   → Action Needed: Strengthen all three dimensions
                    (not just one)
   ```

4. **Regional Comparison (No Map Yet)**

   Simple bar chart comparing Tanzania with:
   - East Africa average
   - Southern Africa average
   - Global average

   Shows relative positioning

5. **Scenario Analysis**

   Interactive tool showing:
   ```
   IF Vulnerability reduced by 20%
   THEN Risk drops from 4.2 → 3.7

   IF Coping improved by 30%
   THEN Risk drops from 4.2 → 3.5

   IF Both improved
   THEN Risk drops from 4.2 → 3.1 (Medium)
   ```

6. **Teaching Box:**
   ```
   📌 FINAL KEY LESSON
   ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
   Risk Is Multi-Dimensional

   ❌ WRONG: "Tanzania is high risk - we can't change that"
   ✅ RIGHT: "Tanzania has medium-high risk across three
              dimensions - all can be reduced"

   Actions:
   1. Reduce Exposure:  Land-use planning, relocation
   2. Reduce Vulnerability: Development, poverty reduction
   3. Increase Capacity: Early warning, infrastructure

   → Risk is MANAGEABLE, not fixed
   ```

**Example: PMO-DMD (Prime Minister's Office - Disaster Management Department)**

- National risk profiling
- Resource allocation planning
- Preparedness prioritization
- Multi-hazard contingency planning

- Identifies highest-risk areas for pre-positioning
- Justifies budget allocation
- Informs national DRR strategy

| **GST** (Geological Survey) | Earthquakes, landslides, volcanoes | Geological hazard zoning, Building code enforcement |

- Provides contextual risk information

- District Councils

- Development planning
- Climate adaptation strategies
- Infrastructure investment prioritization
- Land-use planning

**INFORM Value:**
- Risk-informed development planning
- Avoid creating new vulnerabilities
- Target development to high-risk areas

#### 4️⃣ Humanitarian & Development Partners

**Examples:**
- UN agencies (OCHA, WFP, UNICEF, WHO)
- International NGOs

- Bilateral donors

**Use Cases:**
- Anticipatory action triggering
- Funding allocation
- Preparedness targeting
- Programme design

**INFORM Value:**

#### 5️⃣ Public Users (Limited Scope)
